Our Story

About Future Enlightenment

A community-led charity built on trust, fairness, and care.

Our Mission

Why we exist

Future Enlightenment (FE) was set up in 2009 after listening to the needs of Congolese and Franco-African people living in Hackney. We heard about the difficulties they faced every day — loneliness, language barriers, poor mental health, and difficulty accessing support.

Today, we work across North London to help vulnerable and disadvantaged people — whatever their background — to live better lives and feel part of their community.

Our Vision

A North London where every person — regardless of where they come from — has an equal chance to build a good life and reach their full potential.

Our Mission

To promote social integration, equality, and inclusion by supporting migrants in distress — helping everyone access services, employment, education, and a better life.

Our Principles

How we work, every day.

01

Free, always.

No fees, no insurance, no referral needed. The first thing that breaks when life gets hard is access — we refuse to be one more thing in the way.

02

Your language, your pace.

We speak English, French, Lingala and other languages. We meet people where they are, without judgement, and move at the pace that works for them.

03

Community-led, always.

The people we support shape what we do. Lived experience isn't just welcomed — it drives our decisions, our services, and our values.

04

Show up, then keep showing up.

Consistency matters more than expertise. Same place, same welcome, every week — for as long as it helps. That's the whole model.
